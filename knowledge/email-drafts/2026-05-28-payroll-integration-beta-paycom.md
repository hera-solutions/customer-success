## Background

ADMIN-298 is the epic to sync payroll data into Hera via Bindbee. We need 3-5 real customers on Paycom to help us test the integration end-to-end so we can push forward toward the Hera scheduler launch this summer.

The Paycom path has a real customer-side lift before the technical connection can happen. Per Bindbee, the Paycom approval step (NDA, discovery call, MSA add-on, IP allowlisting, credential issuance) usually runs about 10-15 days. We round that to "about two weeks" in the email so we set an honest expectation without committing to a specific number we don't fully control.

This letter goes to the 3-5 Paycom customers John picks from his power-user / closer-relationship list.

Key decisions locked before drafting:

- **Value prop:** Day-one staff sync (new hire in Paycom shows up in Hera same day). Once scheduler ships, time punches line up against scheduled shifts so the customer can catch missed shifts, ghost punches, and timecard issues before payroll runs.
- **Scheduler timing:** This summer.
- **Offer:** Beta tester access + integration cost on us through the beta and for 3 months after general release. The fee is from Paycom (not Hera), believed to be about $25 per month, and shows up on the customer's monthly Paycom bill (not their Hera bill). Mechanic: Hera covers it by issuing a matching credit on the customer's Hera bill each month during the free window. After the free window ends, the credit stops and Paycom keeps billing the customer directly at their standard rate. Use "about" in the email since the exact rate is not yet confirmed.
- **Tone:** Warm, partnership-forward, matching the May 6 MBB thread. No em dashes. No corporate filler.
- **The ask:** Paycom approval step on their side is about two weeks. We get on the call with the Paycom rep alongside the customer and handle the connection on our end (about 20 minutes) once credentials are in hand.
- **Bidirectional sync:** Intentionally not mentioned. One-way only (payroll → Hera) in the pitch.

## Why this is structured this way

- **Opens with a usage-data reason, not generic flattery.** "When we looked at who is using Hera most actively day to day, your team was right near the top of the list" is concrete and earned. The recipient (often a power-user HR/Dispatch lead) knows this is true about them because they live in Hera every day. Makes them feel chosen for a real reason, not blasted with corporate "valued customer" language. Same opener as the ADP version to keep voice consistent across the two campaigns.
- **Reinforces the chosen-group framing.** "Hand-picked from our most engaged accounts" is a second, lighter touch on the same idea — small group, specifically you, not a mass-mailer.
- **Names the feature and timing in the second sentence.** Customers don't have to scroll to find out what this is.
- **Day-one value before the future value.** Same-day staff sync is the concrete near-term benefit. Scheduler-era benefits are framed as "at launch," not "eventually."
- **Honest about the Paycom timeline.** "About two weeks" sets a real expectation without scaring people off. If we tell a Paycom customer "20 minutes" and they hit two weeks of NDA and MSA work, we lose them and we lose trust. This framing protects the relationship up front.
- **Positions Hera as concierge.** "We'll get on the call with your Paycom rep with you, walk through it together" frames us as the partner, not a vendor handing them a checklist. This is the single biggest difference vs. the ADP letter.
- **Offer is on the table near the close.** Beta access, direct line to John, and a clear note that the integration fee is from Paycom (not Hera) and lives on the customer's Paycom bill, not their Hera bill. We cover the Paycom fee through beta + 3 months post-launch by crediting the customer's Hera bill for the same amount each month. After that, the credit ends and the Paycom fee stays on their Paycom bill at the standard rate. The bill-location specificity and the explicit credit mechanic protect us from "wait, I thought this was free" and "how exactly are you covering it" conversations later.
- **"About" before the dollar figure.** The exact Paycom rate is not 100% confirmed on our end. "About $25 per month" sets a real expectation without locking us into a number we may need to adjust.
- **Ask at the bottom is one action and references the Paycom side explicitly.** "So we can map out the Paycom side and get you started" reminds them there's a sequence and we're going to drive it.
- **Honest exit ramp.** "If the timing isn't right, just let me know."
- **No em dashes. No "I hope this finds you well." No "circle back." No "leverage."**

## Things NOT to do in this reply

- Don't promise "20 minutes total" or hide the Paycom approval step. The Bindbee documentation and our own KB confirm the customer-side lift is real. Hiding it now will cost us the relationship later.
- Don't mention bidirectional sync. Same reason as the ADP draft.
- Don't promise an exact scheduler launch date. "This summer" is the strongest specific we can stand behind.
- Don't drop in the three Hera IP addresses or the NDA / MSA mechanics in this email. The detail belongs on the call, not in the intro. We have a KB article for it.
- Don't link the Paycom KB article in this email. It's an internal-leaning doc and reads heavier than the soft "we'll walk through it together" framing we want here.
- Don't oversell the 20-minute connection number. It only applies once credentials are issued and IPs are allowlisted.
- Don't imply Hera charges the integration fee. The fee is from Paycom. We are covering it during beta + 3 months, but the ongoing cost after that is between the customer and Paycom. Be explicit about this so it's never a surprise.
