## Why this is structured this way

- **Apology is one sentence and specific.** "I was out last week and just got caught back up" gives a real reason without over-apologizing or sounding defensive.
- **Matthew's role is named, not just CC'd silently.** Mike learns why Matthew is there in the same sentence Matthew appears, which avoids the "who's this new person" reaction.
- **The status update leads with what's done, then the gap.** Finalized on our side first, then the honest "not quite ready for true testing" so Mike isn't left guessing why we're asking him to pause.
- **The ask is concrete and small.** Turn API Central off. One action, with the reason included so it doesn't read like busywork.
- **The connection-issue note is informational, not a commitment.** John says it's solved on his side and that he'll reach back out when it's time to walk through it. No date promised, because none is firm.
- **Close is light.** "Thanks for your patience" once, no filler.
- **No em dashes. No "I hope this finds you well." No "circle back."**
