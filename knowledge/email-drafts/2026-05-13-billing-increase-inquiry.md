## Draft reply

**Subject:** Happy to walk you through your billing

Hi [Name],

Thanks for raising this. A jump in your bill without a clear reason is frustrating, and I want to make sure you walk away from this knowing exactly what you're paying for. Let's get on a call so we can go through your account together and confirm the billing matches how you're actually using Hera.

A quick overview so you can start looking before we talk:

- Hera bills **$9 per Active associate, per month**, at the end of the month for the month that just ended.
- Only associates in **Active** status are billable. Anything Inactive (Terminated, Medical Leave, Personal Time / Vacation, Failed Onboarding, Misc) and Onboarding statuses are free.
- So when a bill goes up, it's almost always because the count of Active associates has gone up. Either you've genuinely added people, or some associates are still set to Active who shouldn't be.

The fastest thing to check on your end is your Associate List in Hera (Associate Management > Associate List). Anyone listed as Active who is no longer with you should be moved to Inactive (Terminated). Those associates only count toward billing while they're Active, so updating today stops new charges right away.

If we find that any associates stayed Active longer than they should have on past bills, we'll work through that with you directly and make it right.

For the call, [paste scheduling link OR propose 2-3 specific times this week]. I'll have your account pulled up so we can walk through it line by line.

Best,
John

## Why this is structured this way

- **Acknowledges the experience first.** A bill going up without explanation is unsettling, and they deserve to hear that landed before we explain anything.
- **Gives them the call they asked for, fast.** That's the specific ask in their message. Everything else is in service of making the call productive.
- **Equips them to self-check.** They'll feel better walking into the call knowing what to look for. Most customers will spot stale Active statuses on their own.
- **Reassures on historical billing without pre-committing to a credit.** Saying "we'll make it right" is a soft promise that respects the customer without binding us before we look at the data.
- **No legalese, no filler.** No "we appreciate," no "per our policy," no em dashes.

## Things NOT to do in this reply

- Don't apologize for the bill itself if no error is established yet. The bill may be correct.
- Don't pre-commit to a specific credit amount before reviewing the account with Matthew or with the data in hand.
- Don't blame the customer for not updating their Associate List. Lead with the system explanation, not user error.
- Don't list every status individually as a wall of definitions. The point is: Active is the only billable one. The detail can come on the call.
