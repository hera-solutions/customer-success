# Template: Customer asking about fees, charges, or a billing increase

Use this when a customer reaches out because they're surprised by a charge, asking how fees work, or noticing their bill has gone up. The customer is not necessarily upset — they want clarity. Lead with clarity, not defense.

For a real example using this template, see [`email-drafts/2026-05-13-billing-increase-inquiry.md`](../email-drafts/2026-05-13-billing-increase-inquiry.md).

## Structure

1. **Acknowledge the customer's experience.** One short line that says you hear them. Skip if the customer's message is purely a calm "how does this work" — match the energy of the inbound.
2. **Offer the call (if they asked for one).** Don't bury it. They asked, give them yes.
3. **Plain-language billing model.** Three bullets max:
   - $9 per Active associate, per month, billed at the end of the month.
   - Only Active is billable. Every other status is free.
   - A bill going up usually means more associates are sitting in Active.
4. **Self-check action.** Tell them exactly where to look (Associate Management > Associate List) and what to do (move anyone Inactive who is no longer with them).
5. **Reassurance on historical billing.** "If we find associates stayed Active longer than they should have, we'll work through that with you directly and make it right." Soft commitment, not a pre-promised credit.
6. **Specific call options.** Scheduling link or 2-3 proposed times. Not "let me know when works."
7. **Sign-off.** Plain. Just "Best, [name]."

## Subject line options

- Happy to walk you through your billing
- Let's go through your billing together
- Quick context on your billing, and let's get on a call

Match the customer's tone. Skip "Re: Billing question" — too generic.

## Tone rules

- Plain English. No legalese, no corporate filler.
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or hyphens.
- Customer-forward. Open with their experience, not Hera's process.
- Be specific. Name the navigation path. Name the dollar amount. Name the statuses.
- One purpose per email. The purpose is to get the call on the calendar and prime them with context.

## What to never do

- Don't apologize for a charge that may turn out to be correct. "Sorry for the inflated bill" pre-commits you.
- Don't pre-commit to a credit amount before reviewing with Matthew or pulling the data.
- Don't blame the customer for stale Active statuses. Lead with the system mechanic, not user error.
- Don't drop a wall of every status definition. "Active is the only billable status, everything else is free" is enough for the email. Detail comes on the call.
- Don't say "we appreciate you reaching out" or "we take this seriously." Filler.

## Variables you fill in

- `[Name]` — customer's first name. Look it up in Intercom or their email signature.
- `[scheduling link OR specific times]` — Calendly link if you have one, otherwise 2-3 concrete options.

## When to escalate to Matthew before replying

- Customer is asking about a specific credit they were promised by Matthew.
- Customer is at risk of churn over the bill (uses words like "this is the last straw," "looking at alternatives," "considering canceling").
- Bill increase is dramatic (e.g., doubled or more month-over-month) and you can see in Zoho that an existing discount may have rolled off.

In those cases, loop Matthew in before sending the response, or send a holding reply and follow up after talking to him.
